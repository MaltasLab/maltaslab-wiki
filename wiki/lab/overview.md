# Lab Overview

## Research focus

The Maltas Lab uses tools from statistical physics, population genetics, evolutionary game theory, and computational biology to understand how microscopic growth processes and genome dynamics at the single-cell level give rise to collective behaviors observable at the population level.

## Model systems

- *Enterococcus faecium* — antibiotic resistance evolution experiments
- *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* — experimental evolution
- Non-small cell lung cancer — evolutionary dynamics in cancer

## Core questions

The lab seeks to understand, forecast, and ultimately control evolutionary dynamics across diverse biological scenarios. Current focus areas include:

- **Frequency-dependent interactions** between sub-populations
- **Evolution of collateral sensitivity** — how resistance to one drug affects sensitivity to others
- **Predicting evolution in multi-drug environments**

## Theoretical approach

Through specific experimental and computational examples, the lab aims to uncover unifying theoretical principles applicable across systems. Work spans from wet-lab experimental evolution to purely theoretical population genetic studies (e.g., fixation time under frequency-dependent selection).
