## [2026-04-05] ingest | Neidhart2014_RMFModel

Ingested Neidhart, Szendro & Krug 2014 (Genetics, doi:10.1534/genetics.114.167668), a non-lab non-canonical theoretical paper introducing the simplified single-parameter rough Mount Fuji (RMF) fitness landscape model and providing comprehensive mathematical analysis. Key results: explicit formulas for number of local maxima ($\mathcal{M} \approx 2^L/(L\cosh(c)+1)$), location of global maximum, and fitness correlation function (unique property: goes negative for $r > L/2$); for Fréchet-class (heavy-tailed) randomness, the fitness gradient is asymptotically irrelevant to ruggedness; adaptive walk length depends strongly on initial Hamming distance $d$ to reference sequence even when single-step statistics are $c$-independent. Crucially: the paper identifies that the RMF model was **originally introduced by Aita et al. (2000)**; Neidhart2014 simplified it to a single parameter. This corrects previously incorrect attributions in the wiki (fitness-landscapes and genetic-epistasis hubs) that credited Maltas2020 with introducing the RMF model — Maltas2020 *uses* the Neidhart2014 version. ## [2026-04-05] ingest | NguyenBa2019_TravelingWave

Ingested Nguyen Ba, Cvijović, Rojas Echenique, Lawrence, Rego-Costa, Liu, Levy & Desai 2019 (Nature 575:494–499), a canonical non-lab paper introducing a renewable DNA barcoding system (3 orthogonal Cre-Lox sites, fresh barcodes assigned every 100 generations) to track lineage ancestry across 1,000 generations in diploid *S. cerevisiae* (Ne ~$5 \times 10^6$). Central findings: first direct empirical demonstration of a traveling wave of adaptation (broad stable fitness distribution around rising mean); leapfrogging (low-fitness lineages with rare strong mutations overtake higher-fitness lineages, refuting the assumption that the future common ancestor is always the current fittest); rich-get-richer effect (high-fitness lineages expand to larger sizes, lowering drift and granting access to the full DFE including weak-effect mutations, while low-fitness lineages are restricted to large-effect mutations). Note: source PDF named DesaiTravelingWave.pdf (corresponding author); Nguyen Ba, Cvijović, and Rojas Echenique are equal co-first authors; cite key NguyenBa2019 follows first-author convention. Updated dna-barcoding-lineage-tracking, distribution-of-fitness-effects, and fitness-landscapes topic hubs; updated lab/overview.md.

## [2026-04-05] ingest | Bakerlee2022_IdiosyncraticEpistasis

Ingested Bakerlee, Nguyen Ba, Shulgina, Rojas Echenique & Desai 2022 (Science 376:630–635), a canonical non-lab paper constructing a near-complete 10-mutation, 1024-genotype *S. cerevisiae* fitness landscape in 6 environments using a hierarchical CRISPR gene drive system. Central finding: fitness-correlated trends (FCTs) — the hallmark of apparent global epistasis — arise from just ~4 idiosyncratic pairwise interactions on average (out of a median 53 total terms), not from non-specific global fitness coupling; FCTs disappear when genotypes are partitioned by 2–3 interacting background loci. Note: source PDF named DesaiEpistasisLandscape.pdf (corresponding author); equal co-first authors Bakerlee and Nguyen Ba; cite key Bakerlee2022 follows first-author convention; paper is a mechanistic bridge between Kryazhimskiy2014 and Ardell2024 (same corresponding author Desai). ## [2026-04-05] ingest | Cvijovic2015_MutationFluctuating

Ingested Cvijović, Good, Jerison & Desai 2015 (PNAS, doi:10.1073/pnas.1505406112), a canonical non-lab theoretical paper deriving the fixation probability of a new mutation in a two-state fluctuating environment. Central result: the modified Kimura formula $p_\text{fix} = 2\bar{s}/(1 - e^{-\bar{s}/s^*})$ with an elevated drift barrier $s^*$ that depends on N, within-epoch selection $s$, mean epoch length $\tau$, and epoch variability $\delta\tau$. Key finding: in the intermediate regime $1 \lesssim s\tau \ll 2\log(Ns)$ — directly relevant to bacteria with $N \sim 10^6$ under daily drug cycling — selection efficiency is dramatically reduced and deleterious mutations can fix more readily than equivalent always-beneficial mutations; "seasonal drift" from variable epoch lengths decays the drift barrier only logarithmically with N. Note: source PDF named CvijovicFluctuating.pdf (first author); Cvijović and Good are equal co-first authors; Desai is corresponding author. Note: volume and pages not extracted from conversion — [VERIFY] from published article. Updated evolution-in-fluctuating-environments and distribution-of-fitness-effects topic hubs; updated lab/overview.md.

## [2026-04-05] ingest | Kryazhimskiy2014_GlobalEpistasis

Ingested Kryazhimskiy, Rice, Jerison & Desai 2014 (Science 344:1519–1522), a canonical non-lab paper establishing global diminishing returns epistasis as the dominant form of epistasis in *S. cerevisiae* adaptation. Key experiment: 432 diversification lines → 64 Founder genotypes spanning a range of fitness → 640 replicate adaptation populations (10 per Founder) for 500 generations; 104 whole-genome-sequenced clones + targeted knockouts of *GAT2*, *WHI2*, *SFL1* in 18 genetic backgrounds. Key finding: Founder fitness explains 46% of variance in fitness increment; specific Founder genotype explains only 4%; lines from same Founder share no more mutations than chance — ruling out modular and idiosyncratic epistasis; diminishing returns confirmed by targeted knockouts with no background-specific deviations. Note: source PDF named SergeyDesaiEpistasis.pdf (two most prominent authors); Kryazhimskiy is equal-corresponding author with Desai; cite key Kryazhimskiy2014 follows first-author convention; this paper is the conceptual precursor to Ardell2024 (same last author, extends to environment-invariance). Updated genetic-epistasis, distribution-of-fitness-effects, and fitness-landscapes topic hubs; updated lab/overview.md.

## [2026-04-05] ingest | Good2017_MolecularEvolutionLTEE

Ingested Good, McDonald, Barrick, Lenski & Desai 2017 (Nature 551:45–50), a canonical non-lab paper constructing a time-resolved molecular fossil record by whole-genome metagenomic sequencing of 1,431 samples at 500-generation intervals across 60,000 generations of all 12 LTEE *E. coli* populations. ## [2026-04-05] ingest | Ardell2024_GlobalEpistasis

Ingested Ardell, Martsul, Johnson & Kryazhimskiy 2024 (Science 386:87–92), a canonical non-lab paper measuring fitness effects of ~100 barcoded insertion mutations across 42 *S. cerevisiae* strains in 6 environments. Key finding: global epistasis slopes are nearly environment-invariant (87% of pairwise comparisons statistically indistinguishable); the environment tunes only one effective parameter, the pivot growth rate $\bar{\lambda}_e$, yielding the generalized global epistasis equation $\Delta\lambda_{mge} = b_m(\lambda_{ge} - \bar{\lambda}_e) + \eta_{me} + \xi_{mge}$; DFE moments collapse onto universal environment-independent curves when adjusted for the pivot. ## [2026-04-05] ingest | Michel2008_MultidrugsResistance

Ingested Michel, Yeh, Chait, Moellering & Kishony 2008 (PNAS), a canonical non-lab paper extending the mutant selection window (MSW) concept to two-drug combinations in *S. aureus* via high-throughput scanner-based colony imaging and a mathematical model decomposing $F_{XY}(C_X, C_Y)$ into single-drug resistance frequencies, drug epistasis $\eta_{XY}$, and cross-resistance $\xi$ (one free parameter).
